AI Academic Advisor - Course Planning Agent for Chicago Booth

LangGraph · RAG · OpenAI · FAISS · Streamlit · 2025

Built with Arnav Agrawal as the final project for the Generative AI course at UChicago, taught by Prof. Michael Spertus. Booth MBA students spend significant time searching across portals, Slack, and scattered PDFs to find course information, bidding strategy, and policy details. We built a single AI-powered assistant that answers all of it - from "What are the prerequisites for course 30000?" to "Is this course worth taking?" to "How should I bid in Phase 2?"

The system is a LangGraph pipeline with an LLM router that classifies queries into intents (course facts, sentiment, bidding, policy, planning), then dispatches to deterministic tools for structured lookups (bid history, prerequisites, schedules, evaluations) and RAG over curated policy documents and student reviews - running in parallel. A synthesizer LLM combines both outputs with citations, preferring tool data for factual queries and RAG for policy context. Key design decisions included keeping the app stateless to prevent hallucination, adding an LLM-based course resolver to prune invalid IDs before tool execution, and implementing keyword search as a fallback when vector retrieval fails.

Mint — AI Sports Card Discovery

React · Vite · AI Recommendations · 2026

Built as my final project for the UI/UX Design course in UChicago's MS in Computer Science program, taught by Prof. Andre Marques.

The sports trading card market is valued at $10B+ and growing, but the collector experience is fragmented across 5-6 platforms. I conducted user research with collectors ranging from casual hobbyists to investor-collectors, identified discovery as the core bottleneck, and built Mint — an AI-powered platform that personalizes card recommendations, surfaces market intelligence, and tracks collection value.
